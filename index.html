Negative TB Test: within one calendar year

Certificate of Clearance from the California Commission on Teacher Credentialing (CTC)

How to apply for your Certificate of Clearance

  • Print a copy of Livescan 41-LS form
  • Submit the Livescan 41-LS form and processing fee to the Livescan operator when the prints are taken.
  • A list of locations that offer Livescan services are found here
  • After you complete the Livescan, log on to the CTC Online Direct Application Site

Your Certificate of Clearance will be verified by an admission coordinator on the CTC website.

GPA: A minimum 2.75 GPA in the last 60 semester/90 quarter units is required for admission into the CCOE credential program. Applicants must also meet all university Title V GPA requirements.

GPA Alternatives

If you do not meet the 2.75 GPA requirement, but have earned a 2.5 GPA or better, you may submit a Special Action Petition for committee consideration. Proof of meeting the basic skills requirement is strongly recommended prior to completing a special action petition.

Basic Skills Requirement: At a minimum, attempt of all three sections of the CBEST

Basic Skills Alternatives

For admission with conditions, you must show proof of one of the following:

  • Attempt of all three sections of the CBEST
  • Passing scores from the CSET: Writing Skills text plus ALL three subtests of the CSET Multiple Subjects
  • Passing scores from the CSU Early assessment Program (EAP), English and Math, or CSU English Placement (EPT) and Entry Level mathematics (ELM) Test.
  • Proof of passage of CTC-approved out-of-state basic skills exam.
Click here for CTC exam information.

Applicants must bring original test scores to the Office for Student Services, King Hall D2078 to receive credit for the basic skills requirement.

Note: Successful completion of the Basic Skills Requirement is a requirement for progressing on to fieldwork and meeting the requirements for credential recommendation.
